A Tropical Fusion Born in the Visayas

Buko Pandan is believed to have originated from the Visayas region, where buko (young coconut) and pandan leaves are abundant and frequently used in Filipino cooking. Pandan leaves, with their sweet floral aroma, have long been a staple in Southeast Asian cuisine. Filipinos used it to flavor rice, kakanin (rice cakes), and even drinks. But it was in the mid-20th century that someone had the brilliant idea to combine pandan-flavored gelatin with young coconut and cream, creating what is now one of the most beloved cold desserts in the country. Over time, the recipe evolved to include sweetened cream, condensed milk, and sometimes sago pearls or kaong, making it richer and more indulgent.

Recipe & Ingredients

Ingredients

  • 2 cups young coconut meat (buko), shredded
  • 1 pack (25g) green gelatin powder (pandan-flavored) or plain gelatin + pandan extract
  • 2 cups water (for the gelatin)
  • 1 cup all-purpose cream
  • 1/2 cup condensed milk (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup nata de coco or kaong (optional)
  • Pandan leaves (if using fresh leaves for flavor)

Instructions

  1. Dissolve the gelatin powder in water and cook according to the package instructions.
  2. If using plain gelatin, simmer with pandan leaves or add a few drops of pandan extract.
  3. Pour into a tray and let it set. Once firm, cut into small cubes.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, combine:
    • Buko strips
    • Gelatin cubes
    • All-purpose cream
    • Condensed milk
    • Optional: nata de coco or kaong for texture
  5. Mix well and adjust sweetness as desired.
  6. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours (overnight is even better!).
  7. Serve cold in cups or bowls.
