Recipe & Ingredients

Ingredients

  • 2 cups finely ground glutinous rice flour (galapong)
  • 1 cup water
  • ¬Ω cup white sugar
  • A pinch of salt
  • Coconut milk (optional, for added richness)
  • Oil for deep-frying
  • Perforated coconut shell or tin shaker (or make your own using a clean can with small holes)

Instructions

  1. Mix rice flour, sugar, salt, and water (or coconut milk if using). The batter should be slightly thick but pourable.
  2. Traditional cooks use a halved coconut shell with holes poked through the bottom.
  3. You can also use a homemade tin shaker (an empty condensed milk can with holes) to recreate the same effect.
  4. In a deep frying pan or wok, heat the oil over medium.
  5. Shake or swirl the batter into the hot oil in circular or spiral motions. The batter will form intricate patterns and crisp up quickly.
  6. Once golden and crisp, remove the tinagtag and drain on paper towels. Let cool and crisp up more as it rests.
