About Salukara

A Taste of Waray Heritage

Salukara is believed to have originated even before the Spanish colonization of the Philippines, when early Filipinos were already fermenting rice to make food that was nutritious, filling, and easy to cook. The fermentation method gave the dish a slightly sour flavor, much like Japanese sourdough pancakes or Ethiopian injera‚Äîproof that Filipino food has long embraced complexity. The name ‚ÄúSalukara‚Äù comes from the Waray term for this rice pancake, which was often cooked in the morning for breakfast or served to guests during family gatherings and special occasions. It was often made in households using traditional tools: burnay jars for fermenting, woodfire stoves, and banana leaves as both cooking liner and natural flavor enhancer.
